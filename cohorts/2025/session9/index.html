Who the hell am I?

Jamshid Hashimi, a software engineer and entrepreneur, holds degrees in Computer Engineering and Innovation Management. He founded CodeWeekend, Afghanistan’s largest developers’ community, training over 1,000 students. As a Chevening Scholar, he’s recognized for impactful e-government and software solutions.

Hashimi champions tech education through CodeWeekend’s coding sessions and bootcamps. His Stack Overflow contributions (7,775 reputation) and fundraising, like the BMO Vancouver Marathon, highlight his community focus. Colleagues praise his leadership and ability to inspire innovation.

Based in Vancouver, Hashimi leads RapidIteration, delivering global software solutions. His consultancy work includes award-winning digital platforms and teaching coding to beginners. His multicultural background drives collaboration and social good in tech.
